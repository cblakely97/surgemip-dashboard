STOFS2D-Global for SurgeMIP

About Progress Station Comparison

Project Overview

This project contributes to SurgeMIP, a model intercomparison project for global storm surge simulation. We run the STOFS2D-Global mesh in ADCIRC to produce multi-decade water level hindcasts and climate projections, enabling analysis of extreme coastal water levels under both historical and future climate conditions.

Model Configuration

Timestep6 seconds

CFS/CFSv2 Reanalysis

1979 – 2024 (46 years)

Historical hindcast driven by NCEP Climate Forecast System atmospheric reanalysis. CFS (pre-April 2011) and CFSv2 (post-March 2011) provide hourly 10m winds, mean sea level pressure, and sea ice concentration at 0.5° resolution. Data sourced from NOAA THREDDS and NCEI.

HadGEM3-GC31-HM Present

1979 – 2014 (36 years) · r1i1p1f1 of 3 ensemble members

CMIP6 HighResMIP present-climate simulation using HadGEM3-GC31-HM (~25 km atmosphere). 6-hourly winds and pressure from the highresSST-present experiment, regridded to the ADCIRC forcing format with pole rotation and 360-day to Gregorian calendar conversion.

HadGEM3-GC31-HM Future

2015 – 2050 (36 years) · r1i1p1f1 of 3 ensemble members

CMIP6 HighResMIP future-climate projection using the same model. Driven by the highresSST-future experiment, providing a paired comparison with the present-climate run under identical model physics and resolution.

Planned Next

EC-Earth3P-HR

~40 km atmosphere · Gregorian calendar · 3 ensemble members

EC-Earth Consortium HighResMIP model. 3-hourly winds and 6-hourly pressure from highresSST-present (1950–2014) and highresSST-future (2015–2050). Standard Gregorian calendar simplifies the forcing pipeline. Multiple ensemble members enable uncertainty quantification.

CNRM-CM6-1-HR

~50 km atmosphere · Gregorian calendar · 10 ensemble members

CNRM-CERFACS (Météo-France) HighResMIP model. 6-hourly winds and pressure. Largest HighResMIP ensemble with 10 members in the future experiment, providing robust statistics on climate change signals in storm surge.

MRI-AGCM3-2-S

~20 km atmosphere · Gregorian calendar · 1 ensemble member

MRI (Japan) super-high-resolution AGCM. The highest atmospheric resolution in HighResMIP (~20 km), particularly valuable for resolving tropical cyclone wind structure and landfall surge. 3-hourly winds, 6-hourly pressure. AMIP-only (prescribed SST).

Other Candidates

CMCC-CM2-VHR4

~25 km · 6-hourly only · 1 member

CMCC very-high-resolution model. Both AMIP and coupled experiments available. Limited to 6-hourly output frequency.

GFDL-CM4C192

~50 km native, ~100 km output · 1 member

GFDL cubed-sphere model. 3-hourly winds but output is regridded to coarser resolution. Has all four HighResMIP experiments.

HadGEM3-GC31-HH

~10 km · 360-day calendar · coupled only · 1 member

Highest-resolution coupled GCM ever run at climate timescales. 3-hourly output but extremely large data volumes. Only available in coupled mode (no AMIP experiments).

Candidate model selection is based on data availability on ESGF as of March 2026. Actual runs depend on compute allocation, data access, and forcing pipeline validation. Not all candidates may be run.

Forcing Data Pipeline

  1. Download — CFS/CFSv2: GRIB2 files from NOAA THREDDS (6-hourly pressure-level archives). CMIP6: NetCDF from ESGF (HighResMIP hist-1950 and highresSST-future experiments).
  2. Convert — CFS: wgrib2 extracts surface variables, merges to monthly NetCDF. CMIP6: xarray-based regridding with 360-day to Gregorian calendar interpolation.
  3. Format — Output as ADCIRC-compatible NetCDF: fort.221 (pressure), fort.222 (wind), fort.225 (ice). Hourly for CFS, 6-hourly for CMIP6.
  4. Run — Each simulation year runs as a separate PBS job, hotstarting from the previous year. Coldstart includes a 10-day tidal ramp-up.

Validation

Model output is compared against tide gauge observations from the GESLA v4 dataset (Global Extreme Sea Level Analysis). For each station within 50 km of a mesh node, we compute correlation, RMSE, and bias after applying a 5-day lagged bias correction to remove mean sea level offsets. Results are shown on the Station Comparison page.
