CMIP6 HighResMIP — HadGEM3-GC31-HM

The CMIP6 scenarios use atmospheric forcing from HadGEM3-GC31-HM, a high-resolution configuration of the UK Met Office Hadley Centre model participating in the HighResMIP protocol. Two paired experiments are run: a present-climate simulation (1979–2014) and a future-climate projection (2015–2050), enabling direct comparison of storm surge statistics under historical and projected climate conditions using identical model physics.

Model Details

HadGEM3-GC31-HM is the high-resolution member of the HadGEM3-GC31 model family. The atmospheric component (Unified Model) runs at N512 resolution (~25 km at mid-latitudes) with 85 vertical levels. The model uses prescribed sea surface temperatures (AMIP-style) in the HighResMIP highresSST experiments.

Experiments

highresSST-present (1979–2014)

AMIP-style simulation with prescribed observed sea surface temperatures and sea ice concentrations. Provides a high-resolution atmospheric reanalysis-like dataset for evaluating the model against observations before using it for projections.

highresSST-future (2015–2050)

Future projection using SST and sea ice fields derived from CMIP6 coupled model projections. The atmospheric model and resolution are identical to the present experiment, isolating the effect of changing boundary conditions on storm surge climatology.

Variables Used

Four variables are extracted from the HighResMIP output and converted to ADCIRC-compatible NetCDF forcing files:

fort.221.ncSea level pressure (psl, E3hr table) — Pa
fort.222.nc10-meter wind (uas + vas, 3hr table) — u and v components in m/s
fort.225.ncSea ice concentration (siconc, SIday table) — fraction 0–1
fort.22Text config listing NetCDF variable and dimension names for ADCIRC

Calendar Conversion

HadGEM3-GC31-HM uses a 360-day calendar (12 months of 30 days each). Forcing data is converted to the standard Gregorian calendar via linear interpolation (nearest-neighbour for sea ice) using make_cmip6_forcing.py. Each output file spans January 1 through January 1 of the following year in Gregorian time, matching the ADCIRC simulation year boundaries.

The wind V-component (vas) is provided on a staggered latitude grid (769 points vs. 768 for other variables) and is regridded to the T-grid before writing.

Data Source

All CMIP6 data is downloaded from the Earth System Grid Federation (ESGF) using download_cmip6.py. Files are retrieved as NetCDF from the ESGF distributed archive.
